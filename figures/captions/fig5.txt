Figure 5. A small movement-independent choice code.

(a) Survivor counts at four stages of the confound cascade applied under the published wheel-plus-body-
pose linear movement control with within-region false discovery rate correction (q < 0.05), pooled
across the Brain-Wide Map
regions: choice-selective, movement-independent, stimulus or prior-independent, and triple-coded
(leading and movement-independent and stimulus or prior-independent). Each horizontal bar gives the
total count (grey) with the superior colliculus (SCm) contribution overlaid (blue); the number to the
right of each bar is the total count, with the SCm count in parentheses. The movement-independent and
stimulus or prior-independent stages are parallel single-confound filters and the triple-coded stage
is their intersection with the leading filter, so the four counts are not a nested sequence. n = 1620
high-firing-rate cells entering the cascade; bars are counts and have no error bars.

(b) Per-cell choice decode AUC before the control (raw deliberation-window rate) and after the
recomputed 4-regressor linear movement control, for the three superior colliculus triple-coded cells,
shown as connected points
(one line per cell). The dashed horizontal line is the 0.57 recoverability bar and the dotted
horizontal line is chance (AUC 0.50). n = 3 cells; points are single-cell AUCs and have no error bars.

(c) Distribution of per-cell choice decode AUC across the population after the recomputed 4-regressor
linear movement control. Grey bars are cells near chance and blue bars are the selective tail (AUC
above 0.65 or below
0.35). The dotted vertical line is chance (0.50) and the dashed vertical lines are 0.35 and 0.65. The
text gives the median AUC and the fraction of cells above 0.65. n = 1620 cells; bars are cell counts.

Colour coding: blue marks superior colliculus cells in (a) and (b) and the selective tail in (c);
grey marks other regions in (a) and the near-chance bulk in (c). Print width 180 mm (double column).
