Figure 4. The decision signal at the boundary, not below it.

(a) Movement-controlled choice decode AUC for each region under the valid linear control (filled blue
circles) and under the over-correcting expanded control (open vermillion squares), for the IBL
Brain-Wide Map (top four rows, bracket labelled IBL) and the Steinmetz 2019 dataset (bottom three
rows, bracket labelled Steinmetz). Horizontal bars are 95 percent bootstrap confidence intervals
resampled over sessions. The dotted vertical line is chance (AUC 0.50) and the dashed vertical line is
the 0.57 recoverability bar. No region's confidence interval lies entirely above the 0.57 line. n is
given as decodable sessions and pooled cells per region: IBL MRN 28 sessions, 356 cells; IBL SCm 18,
239; IBL IRN 9, 97; IBL GRN 6, 86; Steinmetz MRN 7, 223; Steinmetz SC 6, 168; Steinmetz SNr 4, 79. IBL
SNr is omitted because only one session is decodable.

(b) Number of choice-selective cells, of 301 pooled across IBL regions, whose choice signal remains
significant after movement control, for three controls: expanded (over-removes), linear (valid
middle), and pca (under-removes). The dashed line marks the linear count. Bars are raw survivor counts
(per-cell choice-AUC permutation test, p < 0.05, 2000 permutations) and have no error bars.

Colour coding: blue marks the valid linear control and vermillion marks the over-correcting expanded
control in (a); in (b) vermillion marks expanded, green marks linear, and orange marks pca. Markers in
(a) are distinguished by shape as well as colour (filled circle versus open square). Print width 180
mm (double column).
