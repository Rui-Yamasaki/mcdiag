Figure 2. The step-versus-ramp identifiability map.

Throughout, each recovery rate is the fraction of correctly recovered generators over R = 40 repeats,
and no error bars are shown.

(a) Recovery map under realistic negative-binomial emissions (overdispersed, Fano >= 1.5; ramp
diffusion sigma = 0.4). Heatmap of the step-versus-ramp recovery rate (cividis colour scale from 0.50
to 1.00) over mean firing rate (y axis, Hz) and pooled trial count N (x axis). The white dashed contour
marks the 0.80 recovery level. The circled point with a callout is the IBL single-unit operating point
(about 40 trials at 20 Hz, recovery 0.56), which would require pooling to N of about 320 to reach 0.80.

(b) Recovery rate versus pooled trial count N at 20 Hz on the same data, for two fitters: a misspecified
Poisson fit (grey squares) and a matched negative-binomial fit (green circles). At N = 40, 160, 320 the
Poisson values are 0.48, 0.53, 0.52 and the negative-binomial values are 0.56, 0.73, 0.80. The dotted
line is chance (0.50) and the dashed line is the 0.80 target.

(c) Recovery rate versus ramp diffusion sigma (firing rate 20 Hz, pooled K = 50), for three series:
N = 320 pooling both families (grey circles), N = 160 both families (dark squares), and N = 160
true-ramp trials only (orange dashed triangles). The dotted green vertical line marks the canonical
sigma = 0.4; the dotted line is chance (0.50) and the dashed line is the 0.80 target.

(d) Probability that the step model is selected versus generator stepiness (0 is pure ramp, 1 is pure
step). Faint blue lines are individual pooled-N curves and the bold blue line is their average. The
grey band marks the non-identifiable region where step and ramp are not distinguishable; the
step-selection probability crosses 0.5 near stepiness 0.78.

Colour coding: cividis encodes the continuous recovery rate in (a); grey marks the misspecified Poisson
fit and green the matched negative-binomial fit in (b); blue marks the step model and orange the
ramping family throughout. Print width about 180 mm (double column).
