Figure 6. Single-trial dynamics recovery is out of reach at current yields.

(a) Maximum number of simultaneously recorded good-QC units per region across the full IBL Brain-Wide
Map cohort, for the five decision-core regions ordered by their maximum. Bars are coloured by region.
The dashed red line is the 120-cell requirement for population step-versus-ramp recovery. Good-QC units
are clusters carrying the IBL good-unit QC label, at any firing rate. The full cohort is 205 insertions
across 176 sessions, with 259 region-sessions in total (MRN 118, SCm 68, IRN 33, SNr 23, GRN 17), of
which 0 reach 120 units. Bars are counts and have no error bars.

(b) Movement-controlled choice decode AUC for MRN under the valid linear control, for the IBL dataset
(purple) and the Steinmetz 2019 dataset (pink). Error bars are 95 percent bootstrap confidence
intervals resampled over sessions. The dashed line is the 0.57 recoverability bar and the dotted line
is chance (AUC 0.50). n = 28 sessions (356 cells) for IBL MRN and 7 sessions (223 cells) for Steinmetz
MRN.

(c) Single-cell step-versus-ramp recovery rate against pooled trial count N at 20 Hz firing rate under
negative-binomial emissions (Fano 1.5 or greater), from the recovery grid. The dashed line is the 0.80
recovery target and the dotted line is chance (0.50). The green star is the IBL single-unit operating
point (median about 53 trials per session, per-cell choice AUC 0.53). Points are mean recovery rates
over the grid at each N.

Colour coding: in (a) each region has a fixed qualitative colour (MRN green, SCm orange, GRN grey, IRN
purple, SNr blue); in (b) purple marks IBL and pink marks Steinmetz; in (c) black marks the recovery
curve and green marks the IBL operating point. Print width 180 mm (double column).
